E371 — Introduction to Applied Econometrics (undergraduate)
This course introduces applied econometrics with emphasis on both implementation and reasoning. Students learn to translate economic questions into regression specifications, interpret results, and assess whether conclusions are credible. Core methods include linear regression, fixed effects for panel data, binary response models, and instrumental variables, all implemented in R using real datasets. AI tools are explicitly integrated into the course: students are encouraged to use them for coding, exploration, and drafting, while being held responsible for verifying correctness and defending every choice they make. The course concludes with an optional group mini-project in which students use AI to independently learn an econometric method and then teach it to the class with a live data illustration.
 Spring 2025, Spring 2026
E370 — Statistical Analysis for Business and Economics (undergraduate)
This course introduces statistics as a science of understanding and analyzing economic data. Topics include graphical and numerical summaries of data, probability theory, population and sampling distributions, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis. Students develop the tools needed to answer real-world questions and gain a thorough understanding of statistical inference in economics and business contexts. Excel is used for data analysis and computation throughout the course.
 Summer/Fall 2023, Spring/Summer 2024, Summer 2025, Summer 2026
