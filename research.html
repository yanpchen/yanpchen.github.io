Robust Variance Estimation in Linear Regressions: A Projection-Geometry Perspective  [Job Market Paper]
Presentations: North American Summer Meeting of the Econometric Society (2026), CES China Annual Conference (2026), Singapore Economic Review Conference (2026)
Inference in linear regression relies on the plug-in principle: OLS residuals approximate unobserved errors, so residual moments estimate the sampling variance. This principle fails when the residual-maker exhibits non-negligible off-diagonal mass — as occurs with many controls and multi-way fixed effects — causing classical heteroskedasticity-consistent (HC) and cluster-robust variance estimators (CRVE) to understate sampling uncertainty. This paper shows that the variance of the OLS estimator admits an exact finite-sample representation as a Riesz functional of latent covariance blocks, with observable residual moments linked to this target through a linear operator determined by projection geometry. The proposed Riesz variance estimator strictly dominates classical HC and CRVE, remains valid when cluster-specific leverage matrices are singular, and is implemented via a scalable matrix-free algorithm.
