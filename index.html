Welcome to my website!

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Indiana University Bloomington, and I will be on the job market in 2026–2027.

My research develops econometric methods for complex data structures — clustered, network, and panel data — with an emphasis on geometric interpretation. My job market paper builds a projection-geometry framework for robust variance estimation in linear regression that remains valid when classical cluster-robust methods break down. A second strand of my research focuses on network econometrics, spanning both methodology and empirical applications to economic and financial networks.
