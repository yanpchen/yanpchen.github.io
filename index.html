Welcome to my website!

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Indiana University Bloomington, and I will be on the job market in 2026–2027.

My research develops econometric methods for complex data structures, with an emphasis on geometric interpretation. My job market paper uses projection geometry to explain why classical cluster-robust variance estimators fail in modern empirical designs — and to construct an estimator that remains valid when they do not. I am also interested in network econometrics, including both methodology and empirical applications to economic and financial networks.
